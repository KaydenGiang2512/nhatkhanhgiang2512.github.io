What does cloud computing do?

  This branch of technology is relatively young and constantly growing, especially in recent years due to an unexpected surge in need for data storage, synchronization and backup coming from a large majority of the demographic. In order to meet such high demands, the concept of cloud computing was introduced and gradually emerged onto the scene of IT technologies and has established itself as one of the most prevalent advancements in the world. Prior to this, consumers were stuck with conventional forms of data storage as their primary options, which had several inconveniences like insufficient capacity (especially for large enterprises), hefty expenses for buying equipment and hardware installation/repair, dependent on its devices and lack of mobility. Cloud computing came as a fresh new choice that the industry was in dire need for, and it has definitely been living up to the expectations and hype.    

  Nowadays, the term “Cloud computing” is virtually synonymous with data manipulation and almost every single technologically-based operation revolves around it to build their systems. In spite of the revolutionary impact that cloud computing brings about, the core concepts behind this development is not as complicated as people may think. In basic terms, cloud computing refers to the usage of private servers on the Internet for storing, managing and controlling data as opposed to conventional storage methods (RAM, HDD, SSD, USBs, Floppy disks), which is restricted by the local server or internal storage (IS) capacity of the devices that contain them. Moreover, your data will be supervised by a third-party institution, known as a Cloud Service Provider which helps you secure your private information and protect them from being lost or stolen from your typical device storage.    

  One of the greatest advantages that set this innovation apart from its predecessors is the substantial cost-savings on hardware investment and maintenance because the equipment is already purchased by the cloud service provider, so you would only need to pay for the actual service providence. Additionally, the rate of retrieving data while using cloud services is significantly higher, due to its quicker deployment times and fewer steps to acquire the necessary resources. Other distinct benefits of switching to cloud computing include unlimited storage capacity, automatic software integrations, wide-scale collaboration regardless of geographical distances, availability, advanced online security, resilient computing, etc. 
   
  Having discussed the independent advantages that cloud computing offers, I would like to redirect to more recent implementations of this fascinating aspect of technology, such as the integration of cloud computing with mobile apps development. This is by far one of the most ambitious breakthroughs in the entire industry yet one of the cheapest, because it was able to fuse the worldwide pervasiveness and capabilities of our smartphones with the ubiquity and unlimited potential that cloud computing has brought about, without devouring an excessive amount of finance. This so called “Dynamic duo” gave birth to a new movement of cloud-based applications which can store data in vast quantities and can access them for management, backup and retrieval with ease and convenience. Also, this generation of apps are much more versatile in the sense that their data can be stored virtually compared to obsolete apps that rely on device storage, which leads to increase in performance and security.    

  This incredible rise to fame has left many people baffled, with many questions such as: “How did cloud computing hit the mainstream in such a short time?” or “What made cloud computing possible?” or “How will cloud computing reshape our future?”. A surprising fact that I have discovered is that this ideology existed for decades, and not until the late 90’s did it become a reality and by the time the new millennium rolled around, cloud computing was beginning to be used in some technological facilities. The key factor here was the creation of the Internet, which perfectly supported the concept of utilizing online servers to safeguard data instead of fancy hardware. 

What is the likely impact?

  Since its debut a little over two decades leading up to present times, most people have already realized the indispensable impact of cloud computing and its amazing functionalities in our daily lives and in various industries such as healthcare, business, education, apps/games development, e-commerce, logistics and supply chain management, just to name a few. This field of technology is already reshaping our world as we know it by gradually bridging the gap between the countries and people from different cultures. The process of data manipulation and transmission has never been made more available to the general public, and this is just the beginning phase of a major revolution towards further globalization.   
 
  Regarding the future vitality of cloud computing, there is no concrete evidence that can accurately predict the direction of growth that it will take, but experts have begun to make speculations of the possible trends that could become a reality in a few years’ time. According to Data Flair and Tech Republic, 2 of the most renowned websites specializing in data analysis and trend predictions, there are quite a few paths that cloud computing could develop in the following years depending on how the world advances, and what kind of technology will be invented later on.
     
  For instance, a whole sector of occupations relating to the cloud has been added to the IT market in recent years, allowing employers with more flexibility and choice to hire potential candidates to fill these new positions, as well as adding skills requirements to current ones. Some of these include jobs like software engineer, cloud engineer, full-stack developer, data engineer, data scientist, etc. This creates an expansion in job opportunities for technology enthusiasts and more options for a suitable career. Secondly, I previously stated that cloud computing has proven its versatility by working together with other aspects of technology like mobile applications, but this is incomparable to the collaborations we will witness in the future. It is predicted by experts that it is possible to integrate cloud computing into leading edge technologies such as IoT, A.I, robots, machine learning to better enhance artificial intelligence and data communication on a massive scale.

How will this affect you?

   Prior to the popularization of cloud computing, people have had to put up with constant annoyance from their personal devices’ storage, and I am no exception. In the past, I have encountered unfortunate incidents that led to data loss and insufficient storage for important files, which I thought was a serious problem but with no viable solutions at the time. Since switching to the cloud permanently to manage my personal data, I noticed a significant improvement and my life has become much more productive in terms of school materials and assignments/projects files. Cloud computing also make teamwork and large-scale collaborations become considerably smoother and virtually seamless, owing to its ability to handle ridiculous amount of data transmissions all at once.     

  Now that I am so accustomed to the presence of cloud computing in my day-to-day activities, I simply cannot imagine my work life without this fantastic innovation. For one thing, I am sure that my files storage would be absolutely chaotic if I were not using cloud computing to organize my data. Other than that, I feel that using cloud services creates a sense of reliability for me, because I can confidently push my personal data there without fear of my privacy being violated by hackers due to its high security and third-party agreements.     

  In addition, most of my family members are also switching to the cloud as their main method of storing their information, and they unanimously agree that this is by far the best technology for data processing they have ever encountered. The same can be said about my friends, who use cloud services for basically anything nowadays. What they love about cloud computing technology is that it removes the need for hardware like USBs or hard drives, and all you really need to access your data is an online account for the service you are subscribed to.

What does cybersecurity do?

  The other branch of technology that is highly relevant to the success of my app is none other than the field of cybersecurity and privacy. The concept of safeguarding our private data is not new by any means, and as a matter of fact has been around since the early days of modern civilization, when people started to keep secrets about sensitive information and important knowledge that cannot be shared with strangers/untrustworthy individuals. Through time, its importance has only been growing due to constant technological and economical advancements, which led to even more drastic measures taken to guarantee security and prevent unwanted information leaks. Prior to the invention and popularization of the ubiquitous World Wide Web, most of the procedures relating to privacy and data processing was done manually by people, oftentimes resulting in lackluster effectiveness and an ever-imminent threat of attack from criminals who seek those data.    

  After the introduction of the Internet in 1989, humanity witnessed a revolutionary development in the sense of information manipulation and creating an online presence for themselves. Undoubtedly, our lives are tremendously enhanced on account of the unlimited benefits that the Internet provided, but with those advantages came a different threat – hackers/scammers. These cyber criminals found a method to exploit the loopholes and algorithms of the digital world and used that to their advantage to carry out misconducts such as stealing private/crucial data, blackmailing, false advertising, illegal transactions, draining people’s bank accounts without their consent, implanting malware into users’ devices, the list goes on.     

  In order to counteract the current issues and also to prevent potential ones, the field of cybersecurity was created to combat hackers. The principles of cybersecurity are pretty self-explanatory – a system designated to detecting and eradicating malware, harmful viruses and any attempt of hacking/breaching by cyber criminals, and protecting its users and their invaluable data during the process. However, the functionalities of online security are not just restricted to defending electronic devices and deleting viruses, but also entails smaller fields like data encryption, safe transactions, digital trails, supervising online activity, shutting down suspicious websites and ads.    
 
  The indispensable role that cybersecurity holds in our digitized society is more than obvious, with innumerable implementations in our daily lives and an astounding degree of coverage. For example, large-scale operations and enterprises always deploy a cybersecurity department to shield themselves from nefarious attackers with intentions of acquiring important files such as transactions data, income, sensitive files containing confidential information that cannot be revealed to the general public and so on. The same rule applies even for smaller companies and for applications on every platform, where a security system is detrimental to prevent private data from being compromised.                            
  Cybersecurity as a standalone industry has the capabilities to transcend our world for the better, and it is performing marvelously so far. Having said that, when it is integrated with other cutting-edge technologies like A.I, Blockchain, IoT or big data, cybersecurity becomes a much more powerful tool and can navigate the cyber threat landscape with higher efficiency. Using the features that A.I offers, cybersecurity can be upgraded into an intelligent automatic system that constantly scans the web for insights on hackers, cyber threats, on which they devise prevention strategies. Another instance is when online security collaborates with blockchain technology, which results in a fortified layout of defense against any incoming threats. Apart from the relatively secure browsing environment that cybersecurity has, adding the concepts of blockchain ensures safe transactions of all types and maximize privacy of data and transparency.

What is the likely impact?

  Technological development is taking place all around the world at a rapid pace, and the practice of sharing data and knowledge on a virtual platform is becoming stronger than ever. Although this movement obviously has a plethora of benefits to humanity, it also makes our privacy and data easily exposed to nefarious cyberattacks at the same time. Therefore, the vital influence of cybersecurity and its necessity in our current age goes without saying, and that’s part of the reason why this is one of the leading fields of research for developments.      

  Thanks to the full implementation of this incredible aspect of technology, browsing the web has never been more reliable, and the Internet itself is gaining even more optimistic perspective from its users. Nowadays, there is always a sense of reassurance that your device is under protection whenever you visit a new website, download an app, go on social media, making important transactions involving currency, use your mobile devices or having private conversations on messaging platforms. This feeling of security would not have been possible if it were not for those systems keeping watch over our online behavior and sends us warnings of imminent threats. Essentially, the greatest advantage of cybersecurity is that it promotes privacy and guarantees that your digital life is no longer vulnerable to hacking or scamming risks.    

  Furthermore, the expansion of cybersecurity and its subset of branches gradually led to a significant change in the job market, with loads of new occupations introduced by many companies to match the high demand of online security and hacker retaliation. Some of these include information security analyst, chief information security officer, lead software security engineer, penetration tester (white-hat hacker), cyber crime investigator, and so much more. The good thing about this technology is that it does not render an older industry obsolete, but rather elevating them to higher standards in preparation for the future.  


How will this affect you?

  From a personal point of view, to say that cybersecurity improved my life is an understatement because the impact it has on my day-to-day activities are profound. By downloading some authorized cybersecurity software, I am constantly notified of the current state of my personal devices and all traces of anomalies or potential breaches, and of course how to resolve them effectively. The last step is configured to function automatically, regardless of whether I am running these apps or not, which is extremely convenient when I am occupied with other tasks and cannot find the time to manually eliminate these issues.   
  
  The difference that cybersecurity brings to my daily life routines is quite significant due to the fact that I am an IT student, so the majority of my work and communication is largely dependent on the Internet and applications. On account of these security systems, I can confidently use online platforms like these to my own preferences without hesitation, since my privacy and personal data is shielded all the time. Another change is in my behavior while browsing the web, where I have become significantly more cautious because I understand what is at stake, and what kind of scams are present on the Net. Cybersecurity does not simply make your problems disappear, but it also shows you those issues, describe their characteristics and severity, and finally how to recognize and avoid similar threats later on.   

  My family and friends have experienced the benefits of using cybersecurity, and I have to say that they were deeply impressed by the practicality of it. Similar to me, my family loves to surf the web mostly for entertainment purposes like social media, Youtube videos, movies, etc. They mentioned that every time a slight cyber threat was on the horizon, a notification popped up and gave them the option to let the built-in security system eradicate the issues for them, which relieved the headache of having to go through the list of risks and fix all of them one by one. When it comes to my friends, who are also engaged with online activities, say that they have yet to encounter any problems regarding their data’s privacy, and this clearly helped them focus more on their desired plans instead of diverting attention to worry about hackers.     

After a while of scouting Youtube for suitable videos, I managed to find quite a few which were particularly interesting, and helped my gain a better understanding of the actual life that these IT workers lead compared to my previous expectations.

The first video is from Clement Mihailescu, a former software engineer at Google, one of the biggest names in the IT world. Throughout the length of the video, he described what it was like working for Google and what would a typical day at work look like. The thing that sparked my interest was how relaxed the working environment is, despite Google being one of the foremost innovators of technology. Clement shared that tech companies do not typically obsess over being early at work or leaving late at night. In fact, what they really take into consideration is how well you complete your tasks and emphasizes relaxation to boost creativity, not the time you spend sitting at your desk working. His scope of duty is writing code, doing code reviews, designing, participating in group projects assigned by the boss and attending meetings. He is surprisingly interactive with his co-workers, and leads a very active lifestyle. Software engineers like him do not interact too much with customers or clients, because that is not his department. Most of their day is usually spent at the office (working area) with small intervals where they can have a break and grab a quick snack. According to Clement, designing is actually quite challenging for him, because Google takes designing very seriously so standards are very high.

  The second clip is made by TechLead, another ex-employee at Google with the same position. Again, I noticed that Google does not have a fixed working period, so essentially you can come in at any given time and start doing tasks. His work would normally comprise of individual projects (small-scale), and team projects (usually larger in scale) and requires lots of meetings and brainstorming sessions. He also showed the lifestyle you would get as a software engineer at Google, ranging from diet, exercises, leisure activities. 

  The third video I watched was by Joma Tech, a data scientist. As the name implies, his main field of work is analyzing data and graphs, doing presentations on the trends. Other than that, this position shared similarities with the previous ones in terms of teamwork, meetings, brainstorming, in-depth analysis of large amounts of data, and so on. He also showed me the importance of balance between actual work and physical activities to maintain good health and an active mind.

DC CyberSec was the fourth video I viewed. He is a cyber security professional and his day is quite intriguing. He would start his day bright and early, get to work and then check his emails first to see if there are any urgent reports or notices. Then, he would continue researching on relevant subjects to his work before attending a risk meeting to discuss with colleagues about potential threats. The most challenging aspect of his job has got to be coming up with new strategies to improve online security for his clients.

The fifth and final video clip I watched was made by Eduvo Sdn Bhd, an IT technician. His job is dealing with actual customers about complaints or issues they have with their devices. Firstly, the customer service department receives a case, then sends the information to the technician to handle. After that, the technician goes to the customer’s site to troubleshoot and fix the issue. This job is quite different because these people deal with clients in person, so there is more interaction here compared to the previous jobs.
